How it started vs how it's going

Orchestra Auditions Without Sight

Blind orchestra auditions were started to eliminate bias in the hiring process and increase diversity in symphony orchestras. The practice of concealing the identity and gender of musicians from the audition jury was introduced in the 1970s and 1980s in response to discrimination lawsuits and growing concerns about the lack of diversity in orchestras.

To Make Orchestras More Diverse, End Blind Auditions

If ensembles are to reflect the communities they serve, the audition process should take into account race, gender and other factors.
